Cleaning Zangthopalri

Sherubste UN

In an effort to preserve the natural and cultural heritage around Sherubtse College, the Sherubtse United Nations Unit recently organized a community clean-up initiative focused on the Zangthopalri area. This scenic and spiritually significant location holds cultural importance for the Sherubtse community and visitors alike. Through this initiative, the Sherubtse UN Unit aimed to restore the area’s beauty, encourage responsible tourism, and foster a sense of environmental stewardship among students and community members.
Objectives of the Clean-Up Initiative
The primary goals of the initiative were:
Restore Zangthopalri’s Natural Beauty: Clear litter and debris to rejuvenate the environment and enhance the area's appeal.
Promote Environmental Responsibility: Encourage students, faculty, and visitors to respect and preserve local landmarks through responsible waste disposal and eco-friendly practices.
Foster Community Pride: Instill a sense of ownership and pride among Sherubtse students and community members in maintaining shared spaces.
Activities and Implementation
The clean-up initiative involved several activities designed to make a positive impact on Zangthopalri:
Organized Clean-Up Drives: Teams of student volunteers, faculty members, and Sherubtse UN Unit representatives gathered at Zangthopalri, equipped with gloves, garbage bags, and other cleaning supplies. Volunteers worked together to collect litter along pathways, clear away plastic waste, and tidy up areas frequented by visitors.
Installation of Waste Disposal Bins: To support ongoing cleanliness efforts, the Sherubtse UN Unit installed waste bins at key points around Zangthopalri. This encourages visitors to properly dispose of their waste and helps maintain the area’s cleanliness in the long term.
Awareness Campaigns: As part of the initiative, volunteers distributed informational pamphlets and engaged visitors in conversations about responsible tourism and the environmental impact of littering. Signboards were also placed around Zangthopalri, reminding visitors to keep the area clean and respect its cultural significance.
Involving Local Community Members: Local residents were invited to join the clean-up, creating a sense of unity and shared responsibility. This collaboration fostered stronger community bonds and encouraged local support for ongoing maintenance of Zangthopalri.
Impact and Future Plans
The clean-up drive had an immediate positive impact, restoring Zangthopalri’s natural beauty and making it more welcoming for both community members and visitors. Volunteers and community members alike expressed pride and satisfaction in seeing the area returned to its pristine state.
Moving forward, the Sherubtse UN Unit aims to conduct regular clean-up events and engage more students and faculty members. Plans are also in place to promote eco-friendly practices through educational sessions on campus and to organize “Leave No Trace” initiatives that highlight the importance of preserving natural spaces. This ongoing commitment by the Sherubtse UN Unit underscores the college's dedication to environmental stewardship and sustainable community service.
