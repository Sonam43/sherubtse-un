Replacing the toilet dustbins in Sherubste

Sherubste UN
As part of its commitment to fostering a cleaner and more sustainable campus, the Sherubtse United Nations Unit recently spearheaded an initiative to replace and upgrade dustbins throughout the Sherubtse campus. This effort is aimed at improving waste management practices, enhancing campus cleanliness, and encouraging responsible disposal of waste among students and faculty.
Objectives of the Initiative
The key goals of this initiative are:
Promote Campus Cleanliness: Ensure that waste disposal is accessible and convenient by placing new, strategically located dustbins across the campus.
Encourage Recycling and Waste Segregation: Introduce bins designated for different types of waste (such as recyclables, organic waste, and general waste) to promote recycling and proper waste segregation.
Raise Awareness on Waste Management: Educate the campus community on the importance of proper waste disposal and reduce littering on the campus grounds.
Implementation and Activities
The initiative involved a series of carefully planned activities to ensure its effectiveness:
Replacement of Old Dustbins: The Sherubtse UN Unit identified areas on campus where existing dustbins were damaged or insufficient and replaced them with durable, eco-friendly options. These new bins are designed to withstand weather conditions and high usage.
Introduction of Segregated Bins: In key locations, such as cafeterias, libraries, and classrooms, color-coded bins for different types of waste were introduced. This encourages students and staff to separate their waste for easier recycling and disposal.
Educational Campaigns and Signage: The Sherubtse UN Unit ran an awareness campaign to inform students and staff about the importance of using the bins correctly. Clear signage was installed near each bin, guiding users on waste segregation and promoting eco-friendly habits.
Community Engagement: The initiative included engagement sessions with students and faculty to gather feedback and encourage active participation in keeping the campus clean. Volunteers from the Sherubtse UN Unit also conducted waste collection drives to reinforce the habit of using designated bins.
Impact and Future Plans
Since the new dustbins were installed, there has been a noticeable improvement in campus cleanliness. With designated recycling bins in place, the Sherubtse community is gradually adopting better waste disposal habits, contributing to a more sustainable campus environment.
Looking forward, the Sherubtse UN Unit plans to monitor waste management practices continuously and expand the project by organizing regular clean-up drives and introducing composting options for organic waste.
