Frequently Asked Questions

What is Sherubtse United Nations Club?

The Sherubtse United Nations Club is a student organization focused on promoting UN values and activities within the college community.

How can I join the club?

You can join the club by filling out a membership form and attending our meetings. More information is available on our website.

What are the benefits of joining the Sherubtse United Nations Club?

Members gain insights into global issues, participate in various events, and develop skills in diplomacy, leadership, and teamwork.

Does the club organize any events?

Yes, the club organizes events such as workshops, seminars, debates, and Model United Nations (MUN) simulations.

How often does the club meet?

The club holds regular meetings, typically bi-weekly, to discuss upcoming events, ongoing projects, and other club activities.

Who can I contact for more information?

You can reach out to the club’s executive members listed on the 'Our Team' section on the website, or contact us via email provided on the contact page.

Are there any fees associated with membership?

Yes, there is a small membership fee to help support club activities, which will be discussed during the orientation session for new members.

What kind of events can I participate in as a member?

As a member, you can participate in debates, community service projects, leadership workshops, and even international MUN conferences.

How can I stay updated on club events?

Updates are shared through our social media channels and the club’s website. We also announce details in our meetings.

Is there an opportunity to hold leadership roles in the club?

Yes, members have opportunities to run for executive positions or lead specific projects and events throughout the academic year.

Can non-members attend club events?

Yes, some events are open to non-members. However, membership may be required for certain exclusive activities and meetings.
