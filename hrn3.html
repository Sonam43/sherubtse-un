Chorten white Washing

By Sherubtse UN Unit

The annual chorten (stupa) white-washing at Sherubtse College is a cherished event that brings students, faculty, and local community members together in an act of cultural preservation and communal spirit. Traditionally, chortens are sacred structures in Bhutan, symbolizing enlightenment and housing relics, with white-washing being an essential maintenance ritual. This activity involves applying a fresh layer of white limewash to the chortens, a process that renews their appearance and prevents weathering, ensuring they remain beautiful and intact.
Students take pride in participating in this ritual, which is often organized by student clubs or the college’s cultural committee. The white-washing process is not only physically engaging but also spiritually rewarding, as students gain a sense of connection to Bhutanese heritage and contribute to maintaining these sacred structures. In many ways, the event strengthens their cultural identity and instills values of responsibility and respect for tradition.
By coming together to preserve the chortens, Sherubtse College promotes environmental awareness and cultural appreciation, creating a sense of unity among participants. This hands-on experience is an opportunity to pass down Bhutan’s spiritual and cultural legacy, strengthening the bond between generations through a shared act of devotion and respect.
